Question

We want to use our visualization method answer these questions: what are the total death numbers caused by disease in every state in US; is there any difference among total death numbers in different states in US; is there any difference among the numbers of 10 specific leading death reasons in different states in US; is there any difference among death numbers in different years; what is the main cause of people’s death in every states in US.

Data

We choose the data from www.data.gov. This dataset presents the death numbers and age-adjusted death rates for the 10 leading causes of death and all causes in every state in the United States from 1999 to 2016 (link:https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/age-adjusted-death-rates-for-the-top-10-leading-causes-of-death-united-states-2013).

Data are based on information from all resident death certificates filed in the 50 states and the District of Columbia using demographic and medical characteristics.

Design Evolution

We divided our dataset into two parts; the first part is the data of total death numbers in every state in US from year 1999 to year 2016(Total death number was labeled as “All causes” in our dataset); the second part is the data of death numbers caused by 10 specific leading causes in every state in US from year 1999 to year 2016. Then we visualize these two parts separately.

First, when we tried to find a method or visualization to present the total death numbers in every state in US from year 1999 to year 2016(labeled as “All causes” in our dataset), we considered to draw a map at the beginning, but we considered to label numbers of states’ death in the map, but it was not aesthetic if we put text labels in the map; therefore, we decided to use different colors to classify the differences among these numbers. The advantage of using color is that it can make our visualization more eye-catching.

Then I draw two bar charts. The first one is the chart that shows the total death number in every state in US from 1999 to 2016(Total death number was labeled as “All causes”in our dataset); therefore, users can explore the exact number of total death numbers in all states in US, and extract features about the difference among total death numbers in these states. The other bar chart is a little smaller than the first chart. This chart shows the total death number in US from 1999 to 2016, not the numbers in every state. Our dataset contains the data about the total death number in the whole country from 1999 to 2016, so we design this bar chart to show these data.

Second, when we tried to visualize data about number of the death caused by 10 specific reasons in every state in US from year 1999 to year 2016. At first, we considered to draw a bar chart for every reasons, but if we chose this visualization method, we have to draw 10 graphs for 10 specific reasons, so it was not an ideal visualization.

Then we considered to draw choropleth bar chart to present all 10 reasons at the same time, but we cannot present 10 leading causes at the same time, and we have to create two select boxes, one for year and one for 10 leading causes. We tried to convey information in one graph as much as possible, so this method is not an ideal visualization design, either.

After that, we considered to drew the scatterplot graph, but if we chose this visualization method, although I could use the select box to select a specific year and draw circles for every state, I have to use 10 leading causes name as the x axis; because every leading causes name is related to state names, so circles represented for the same state would be drew 10 times in the same graphs. This visualization method would confuse users and readers.

Finally, we decided to draw a parallel coordinate graphs to present all 10 learning causes of death.

We also design a pie chart to present the data about the number of the death caused by 10 specific reasons in US. The radiuses of the arcs vary from the death number caused by these specific reasons to the number; therefore, users can explore the information about the difference of the death numbers caused by these reasons in US.

Implements

We drew a slider, a map, a vertical bar chart, a horizontal bar chart, a parallel coordinate and a pie chart in current state.

First, in the top of our website, there is a slider; therefore, uses can select a year number in this slider through sliding these mouse,. Then the map, two car charts and the parallel coordinate would present the visualization of death numbers in the year that users selected in the slider.

Secondly, we drew a US map, and the color of every state in this map vary from state to state. Through exploring the difference of color of states, users can acquire information about total death numbers in every state in US in the year selected in the select box. When users hover mouse in the state in our map, tooltip would be shown to users, and users can acquire information about state name and the number of “All causes” death from the tooltip.

Thirdly, we design a horizontal bar chart. This chart shows the death numbers in every state in the year selected by the user in the slider. The bars in this chart are sorted by the death number, and the color vary from the number to the number.

Fourthly, we design a vertical bar chart. This chart shows the total number of death number in the whole country, not a specific state. The bar which presented the year that selected in the slider has the color with 0.9 opacity, and other bars which present the years that are not selected have the color with 0.2 opacity.

Fifthly, we drew a parallel coordinate graph to present 10 specific leading causes of deaths. Every axis in the parallel coordinate graph is created for one leading causes, every line is created for a state.

Sixthly, we design a pie chart to present the death number caused by 10 specific reasons in US. The larger the death number is, the larger radius the arc which present this death reason has.
